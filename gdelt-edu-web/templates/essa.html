Key Phrase: Every Student Succeeds Act

The Every Student Succeeds Act or ESSA was passed into law in December 2015 during the Obama administration. The ESSA limited the federal government's role in elementary and secondary education, in comparison to its predecessor, the 2001 No Child Left Behind Act (NCLB). It maintained the standardized tests that were introduced in the NCLB, however it shifted the determination of standards to the states and their school districts. To ensure that states are achieveing their academic goals, the state determined standards are submitted to the Federal Department of Education for review, feedback, and eventual approval.
