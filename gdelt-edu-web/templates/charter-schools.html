Key-Phrase: Charter Schools

This graph displays the occurence of selected keywords within US media article titles from April 1, 2013 to present. Although the GDELT dataset extends to 2005, the SOURCEURL field was not included until April 1, 2013, and this field was used to find the keywords within the titles of the articles. The key-phrase 'Charter Schools' appeared more than any other phrase/keyword with 57985 occurences. I was intrigued and surprised that it was mentioned so much more than any other topic, so I decided to look at the number of mentions and average tone to further delineate its representation within the media.

With average tone of the articles on the left Y axis and number of mentions on the right Y axis, this graph displays several interesting patterns. Firstly, there are two inverse peaks and troughs between the number of mentions and average tone of the articles. The first of these inverse peaks occurs in early 2014, and the other in early 2015. Another interesting pattern is that since 2015 both the number of mentions of charter schools and the average tone steadily decline up until the present.

All three of these patterns suggest a slightly negative portrayal of charter schools in the US media considering that when the average tone peaked (i.e. positive articles involving charcter schools), the number of mentions of the articles reached a trough and when the average tone dipped the number of mentions reached its highest peak since 2014.
